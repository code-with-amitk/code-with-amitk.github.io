East India Company

East India Company

Description
How & when formed 31/12/1600. Formed for exploitation of trade East and Southeast Asia for spices
Powers of East India Compnay Originally Trading body for English merchants for spices. Later added such items as cotton, silk, indigo, saltpeter, tea, and opium
History 1st trading factory etablished in Masulipatnam
Established factories in other parts of India: Bombay, Calcutta (Kolkata), Madras, and Surat.
British eliminate other major trading powers, such as the French, Dutch, and Portuguese, to gain control of India.
1757, overthrew Nawab of Bengal,installing a puppet in his place.
In 1858, the rule of the EIC was transferred to the Crown, in the person of Queen Victoria, and the British Raj began.
The British Raj lasted until 1947, and during this time the British Parliament ruled the Indian subcontinent.
Charter of 1726 Appeals from courts in India went to the Privy Council(a body of advisers appointed by a sovereign or a Governor General) in This way English law system became accepted to Indians
1st establishe in Bombay, Madras, Calcutta
Court of Reqeust: This was created to hear small civil cases. Court used to sit once a week. Judges were from company. it used to give very quick jurisdictions for cases. It can hear cases for 5 pagodas
Choultry Court Established in Madras to hear cases for 20 pagodas, but finally abolished
Charter 1753 1726 the Mayor was appointed by the Aldermen(a member of a municipal assembly or council), but it got changed in 1753
Mayor would be appointed by Governor and council. Aldermen would be appointed by Governor and council
Mofussil faujdari adalat Court of Criminal hearings in all states. Presided by Kazi(helped by Mufti, Maulvies)
Collector had general supervision over the court
Mofussil Diwani adalat Court of Civil hearings in all states. Suits regarding marriage, inheritance, caste, other religious stages
Law of kuran for Mohmedians, Shastras for Hindus were applied.
Regulation act 1773 Act of the Parliament of Great Britain intended to overhaul the management of the East India Company's rule in India
East India Company employees working in India, made huge wealth by bribery and exploitation and purchased shares of East India company
Though EIC was in loss, but these employees were rich and started interfering in politics
Then “Parliament of Great Britain” passed the act
Provisions of Act:
  Company dividends to only 6% and limited 4 year terms for the Court of Directors.
  No employee can take gift or bribery from the native population or engage in personal trade
  Directors need to give information of revenue to company.
  British govt took control over EIC
Defects of Act:
  Court processes were harsh and oppressive.
